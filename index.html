STEVE JOBS

Biography

Steve Jobs was a visionary in the technology industry, co-founding Apple Inc. in 1976 alongside Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. Jobs played a pivotal role in shaping Apple into a global powerhouse, transforming consumer electronics and personal computing markets.
Facing challenges at Apple, Jobs returned in 1997 and led the company to success with iconic products like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. Known for his emphasis on design and innovation, Jobs' impact extended to Pixar Animation Studios, contributing to animated classics like Toy Story and Finding Nemo.
Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011. His legacy lives on through Apple's success, and his contributions to personal computing and consumer electronics remain influential. Jobs' charismatic presentations and the 2005 Stanford Commencement Address are notable aspects of his legacy.
